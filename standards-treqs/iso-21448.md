**Operational	design domain ODD**
Specific	conditions	under	which	a	given	driving	automation	system is	designed	to	function

Conditions may include environmental, spatial, temporal, and system-related factors such as vehicle speed, sensing capabilities, and computing resources.

## 5	 Specification	and	design

### 5.1 Objectives

The purpose of this clause is to achieve the following objectives:

<treqs-element id="f6994569cefe41878f546dce683e40f5" type="compl.clause">

a) contain sufficient information to conduct SOTIF activities.

<treqs-link type="from" target="11e15384a9ee11f0948d467a017f3d7d" />
</treqs-element>

b)	The specification and design shall also be updated after each SOTIF iteration.

<treqs-element id="89db272ec18c11f0a009467a017f3d7d" type="compl.clause">

### 5.2	 Specification	of	the	functionality	and	considerations	for	the	design

The specification and design may include:

- intended functionality and supporting subsystems
- Operational Design Domain (ODD)
- automation level and control authority
- vehicle-level SOTIF strategy
- use cases and transitions
- decision-making logic
- system architecture
- sensor and actuator performance targets
- interactions with:
    - driver and HMI
    - passengers and other road users
    - environment
    - infrastructure
    - cloud, V2X and communications
    - software updates
    - other vehicle functions
- foreseeable misuse
- potential performance insufficiencies, triggering conditions and countermeasures
- warning, degradation and fallback concepts
- data collection and monitoring mechanisms
- operational risk mitigation mechanisms

### 5.4	 Performance	insufficiencies	and	countermeasures	considerations

The design considers potential performance insufficiencies that may cause hazardous behaviour, including:

- insufficient classification
- insufficient measurements
- insufficient tracking
- insufficient target selection
- insufficient kinematic estimation
- false positives
- false negatives
- driving policy limitations

Countermeasures are incorporated into the specification and design to reduce residual risk and improve system robustness.

<treqs-link type="from" target="11e15384a9ee11f0948d467a017f3d7d" />
</treqs-element>

## 6	 Identification	and	evaluation	of	hazards

### 6.1 Objectives

The objectives are to:

- systematically identify hazards arising from intended functionality;
- identify and evaluate the associated risks and hazardous scenarios;
- specify acceptance criteria for residual risk

### 6.3	 Hazard	identification

Hazards caused by functional insufficiencies are systematically identified at vehicle level.

The analysis considers:

- function deviations
- triggering conditions
- reasonably foreseeable misuse
- driver interaction
- hazardous behaviour

Methods from ISO 26262 may support the analysis.

<treqs-link type="from" target="11e15384a9ee11f0948d467a017f3d7d" />
</treqs-element>

<treqs-element id="0031dfdcc24911f08d88467a017f3d7d" type="compl.clause">

### 6.4 Risk evaluation

Risk evaluation:

- assesses hazardous behaviour in specific scenarios;
- evaluates severity and controllability;
- determines whether the risk is acceptable;
- supports specification of measurable safety parameters and validation targets.

<treqs-link type="from" target="11e15384a9ee11f0948d467a017f3d7d" />

### 6.5	 Specification	of	acceptance	criteria	for	the	residual	risk

Acceptance criteria consider:

- regulations
- existing systems
- user risk
- driver performance
- traffic data
- comparable systems
- risk tolerability principles such as:
    - GAMAB
    - GAME
    - ALARP
    - MEM

#### 7.3.1 General

Potential functional insufficiencies and triggering conditions are systematically analysed.

The analysis may use:

* previous experience
* field data
* expert knowledge
* simulations
* qualitative and quantitative methods

It considers:

* performance insufficiencies
* environmental conditions
* foreseeable misuse

### 9.3	 Specification	of	integration	and	testing

The verification and validation strategy:

- demonstrates achievement of SOTIF objectives;
- defines validation targets;
- covers the complete intended functionality;
- includes E/E and other relevant technologies;
- supports monitoring of external data sources;
- specifies validation effort and rationale;
- considers regression testing after functional modifications.
